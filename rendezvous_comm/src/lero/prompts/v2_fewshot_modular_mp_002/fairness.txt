## Fairness constraint (do not violate)

At RL *execution* time, each agent only sees information from its own
sensors plus optional messages. To stay comparable with the ER1 / ER2 /
ER3 / GNN baselines, `enhance_observation` must only read keys that an
agent could actually observe.

- `enhance_observation` may read: `agent_pos`, `agent_vel`, `agent_idx`,
  `lidar_targets`, `lidar_agents`, `messages` (if the task has comms),
  `covering_range`, `agents_per_target_required`, `n_agents`, `n_targets`.
- `enhance_observation` must NOT read: `agents_pos` (of all agents),
  `targets_pos`, `covered_targets`, `agents_per_target`, `all_time_covered`,
  `agents_targets_dists`, `collision_rew`, `time_penalty`.
- `compute_reward` is allowed to read any key, because it runs only
  during centralized training (CTDE). But the induced behavior must
  survive 10M-frame training without reward-hacking; avoid rewards that
  monotonically grow without bound, and keep magnitudes small (the
  framework clips to ±50 for safety).

Violating the observation-side rule makes the result incomparable to
prior work and will be rejected.
