When generating PyTorch reward/observation code, keep all observations strictly local: use only each agent’s own sensors and any explicitly received messages available at execution time. Do not reference global/oracle state, hidden simulator variables, or future information in observation logic.

For rewards, prefer smooth, bounded shaping that encourages coverage progress, task completion, and coordination from local signals. Keep every reward term numerically stable, clamp or scale outputs to stay within |r| <= 50, and avoid sparse spikes, division by zero, NaNs, and dimension mismatches.

If multiple agents are present, compute per-agent outputs independently unless a shared message-based signal is explicitly available. Favor simple tensor operations, explicit shape checks, and deterministic behavior over complex heuristics.
